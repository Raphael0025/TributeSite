Dr Jose Rizal

The man who saved the Philippines

Timeline of Dr. Jose Rizal

  • 1861 - Born from an Inquilino family: On June 19th Jose Rizal is born to become the seventh child born to his parents. Three days later Rizal was christened with the name Jose Protasio Rizal-Mercado y Alonso-Realonda.
  • 1870 - Received a formal education for 2 years in Binan: Rizal returned to Calamba from Binan after studying a year and a half.
  • 1872 - Imprisonment of Doña Teodora
  • 1872 - Rizal entered Ateneo
  • 1877 - Philosophy of letters and Medicine at UST: In June José begins to go to school at St. Tomas University in Manila where he studies philosophy.
  • 1882 - 1st Travel to europe
  • 1883 - He went to Paris for vacation
  • 1887 - Publication of Noli me tangere in Berlin: The publication of Rizal’s novel, Noli Me Tangere, caused for the friars to keepa close eye to Rizal and his family. He was warned by others not to return home ashis life is at sakes.
  • 1888 - 2nd travel to europe to annotate De morga's work: The tension between tenants and Dominican friar because of unpaid taxes andinjustices on Calamba state, which was filed to higher courts, endangered life of Rizal.
  • 1889 - Celebration of Universal Exposition in Paris
  • 1890 - Expulsion of farmers from Hacienda de Calamba
  • 1891 - Publication of El Filibusterismo in Belgium: He left his country shortly thereafter to return to Spain where he wrote a second novel, El Filibusterismo (1891), and many articles in his support of Filipino nationalism and his crusade to include representatives from his homeland in the Spanish Cortes.
  • 1892 - Exile in Dapitan: Rizal returned to the Philippines. While he was in Europe, he joined amovement, Filipino League. As well in Spain, La Solidaridad, where they publishnewspaper to demand reforms. This was then banned.
  • 1892 - HongKong, to build a colony in North Borneo
  • 1892 - Founded La Liga Filipina in Philippines
  • 1896 - Execution of Rizal in Bagumbayan: The Katipunan, founded by Andres Bonifacio, started a revolution againstSpain. This was supported by Rizal. This is also one of the reasons that caused forRizal to be arrested.

--Dr. Jose P. Rizal
